How each tool works — this is the key difference

Link Whisper: WordPress plugin

Link Whisper installs like any WordPress plugin. It runs on your server. The AI scans your content natively — no API keys, no external service, no data leaving your site. When you build a link, it's written directly into your WordPress post content and saved in your database.

Cancel Link Whisper, and every link you built stays. They're in your posts. They belong to you.

Linkbot: SaaS JavaScript snippet

Linkbot is a SaaS platform. You copy and paste a JavaScript snippet into your site's <head> tag. Linkbot's servers crawl your site URLs, identify internal link opportunities, and inject links client-side via JavaScript — not by writing to your WordPress database.

This means Linkbot works on WordPress, Webflow, HubSpot, and other platforms. The tradeoff: it's external infrastructure, not a native plugin.

⚠️ The cancellation problem

When you cancel Linkbot, the links go with it. Their JS snippet becomes inactive. Every link injected by Linkbot stops rendering on your site. The links were never stored in your posts — they were rendered by Linkbot's JavaScript on each page load.

For an agency managing 10 client sites: if a client churns and you cancel Linkbot for their site, every internal link Linkbot built disappears. The client's internal link structure is gone. Your work product evaporates.

Link Whisper writes to the database. Cancel tomorrow. Your links are still there next year.
